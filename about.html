Built by a family that missed the coast

Our story

From a single guesthouse to nineteen suites

Kaia Cove began in 2014 as a four-room guesthouse run by the Rego family on a stretch of cliff nobody else wanted to build on — too steep, too far from the highway, too quiet. Twelve years on, it's exactly what keeps guests coming back.

We grew slowly, one wing at a time, and kept the same rule throughout: no room built without a water view, and no view sold without a room worth waking up in.

What we hold onto

A short list of things we won't change

Nineteen rooms, on purpose

We've had offers to double the property. We haven't, because the cove — and the staff-to-guest ratio — only works at this size.

A local kitchen

Tidewater's menu changes with what the boats bring in and what's ready in the garden, not with a laminated seasonal card.

One host per booking

From your first email to check-out, one person on our team knows your stay — no call centres, no repeating yourself.

The cliff path stays open

The walking path down to the tide pools is free for guests at any hour — it's the reason half of you booked in the first place.
